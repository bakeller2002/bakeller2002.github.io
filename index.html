About

I'm a biomedical engineering BS/MS student at the University of Utah, building machine learning and computer vision systems for physical, real-world problems. My work spans human biomechanics research, data science in numerous modalities, and hands-on problem solving.

Whether the input is motion-capture data, a camera feed, or a clinical signal, I focus on the same things: clean data, thoughtful evaluation, and systems that hold up outside the lab. See what I've built on the projects page.

Objective

I want to build systems that help people make better decisions about the human body. My focus is medical technology and physiological data. Taking the messy, high-dimensional signals that come from people living and moving via sensors and turning them into insight a clinician, researcher, or coach can actually act on. The work I care about starts with high-quality raw measurement and leads to real decisions using pipelines, models, and tools that make complex physiological data trustworthy and usable.

Sports science draws me as one of the sharpest versions of that problem: it sits right at the intersection of data, device, and movement, demanding precise biomechanical measurement, rigorous data engineering, and a human-centered focus on performance and well-being all at once. Optimizing the movement of people who've trained their bodies to the highest level is, to me, both technically fascinating and genuinely worth doing.
